Interested To Learn Few Facts About Bangladesh? Look No Further!

Bangladesh is a small country located in South Asia. It is a country of lush greenery and many waterways. In 1971, then East Pakistan gained Independence from the West Pakistan and thus, a new nation, Bangladesh was formed. Here are 10 facts about Bangladesh:

1. The word, Bangladesh means "Country of Bengal" in local language.

After British India got Independence in 1947, it separated into India and Pakistan. The then East Pakistan wanted their own language and their own territory. After a 9 month war between the East Pakistan and the West Pakistan, the people in the East got independence and Bangladesh was formed. The name itself means a country that speaks Bangla.

Pakistan Army surrendering, 15 December, 1971

2. The population of Bangladesh is 163 millions(est) as of 2016

Bangladesh is highly overpopulated. The numbers keep increasing everyday, which has been a major issue for the country over the years. Interestingly, More than half of this 163 million population is composed of farmers.

A railway station in Bangladesh

3. The Royal Bengal Tiger is Bangladesh's national animal.

The Royal Bengal Tiger is found in a vast forest named "Sundarbans" located in the costal region of Padma, Meghna and Brahmaputra rivers. This majestic creature has a roar that can be heard up to 3 kilometers away. Unfortunately, it is now an endangered species.

4. Bangladesh is sometimes called the Playground of Seasons.

Bangladesh has six separate seasons, which it calls grismo (summer), barsha (rainy), sharat (autumn), hemanto (cool), sheet (winter), and bashonto (spring).

The Playground of Seasons

5. The Bay of Bengal is largest bay in the world

The infamous Bay of Bengal is one of the most famous tourist sights in Bangladesh. It is 2,172,000 square kilometers long. Every year, almost a million people come to visit it, which helps the country grow economically.

6. Cricket is the most popular sport in the country.

Everywhere you go in Bangladesh, you'll always see kids playing cricket, whether it's on the streets or on a ground. In 1999, its national cricket team was part of the World Cup, and in 2000, it received Test status, an honor in the sport for it is the ultimate test of a teams ability and endurance. Bangladesh is widely considered as a country that has the most passionate fans in the world.

7. Bangladesh is the only country to fight a war and declare independence over language.

In 1948, Mohammad Ali Jinnah, the leader of the All-India Muslim league and founder of Pakistan declared in Dhaka that Urdu alone should be the national language of Pakistan. This was strongly opposed by the populace of East Pakistan and following the deaths of students and civilians in demonstrations against it on February 21 1952, Bangla was declared the national language of East Pakistan. UNESCO declared February 21 International Mother Language Day.

8. Bangladesh routinely falls amongst the most corrupt countries in the world.

Public sector corruption in Bangladesh is rampant. The latest data placing it 145th out of 176 in a ranking of the least corrupt nations. Denmark and New Zealand holding 1st place. A study of 149 Bangladeshi parliamentary members conducted by TI Bangladesh in 2012 showed that 97% of the members were involved in illegal activities including 53.5% of members directly involved in criminal activities.

Money is involved in everything.

9. Bangladesh is considered one of the poorest countries in the world

Y 31.5% of a population of 161 million people, still living under the poverty line. That is approximately 50.7 million people, a number that is more than double the entire population of Australia. The wealth gap in Bangladesh is also staggering. Data compiled and published by the World Bank show that the poorest 20% of the population only controlled 8.9% of the countrys total wealth in 2010 and the richest 20% of the population controlled an eye opening 41.5%.

Poverty in Bangladesh

10. Bangladesh has the Largest RIver Delta and Mangrove in the world

Ending with a positive! The Bengal Delta, located on the coastline of Bangladesh is the largest river delta in the world. It is fed by two large Himalayan rivers, the Ganges and the Brahmaputra and a non-Himalayan river, the Meghna which in turn is fed by two other large rivers, the Padma and Januma. The delta is also home to the largest mangroves in the world, the Sundarbans, a UNESCOE World Heritage site covering 10,000 sq. km across Bangladesh and India, 60% falling within Bangladesh.
